# YC Application Questions — Current Batch

Source: extracted from the YC Roaster AI evaluation prompt (`src/lib/ai-judge.ts`), which was built to detect real YC applications vs. other documents.

**Important:** YC updates question wording between batches. Always verify against the live YC application form before your session. If a question here doesn't match what you see on YC's site, use the live wording and treat this file as a structural guide.

---

## Founders Section

1. Who writes code, or does other technical work on your product? Was any of it done by a non-founder?
2. Are you looking for a cofounder?

---

## Company Section

3. Company name
4. Describe what your company does in 50 characters or less
5. What is your company going to make? Please describe your product and what it does or will do.
6. Where do you live now, and where would the company be based after YC? *(Format: City A, Country A / City B, Country B)*
7. Explain your decision regarding location.

---

## Progress Section

8. How far along are you?
9. How long have each of you been working on this? How much of that has been full-time?
10. What tech stack are you using, or planning to use, to build this product?
11. Are people using your product?
12. Do you have revenue?
13. If you are applying with the same idea as a previous batch, did anything change?
14. If you have already participated or committed to participate in an incubator, accelerator, or pre-accelerator program, please tell us about it.

---

## Idea Section

15. Why did you pick this idea to work on? Do you have domain expertise in this area? How do you know people need what you're making?
16. Who are your competitors? What do you understand about your business that they don't?
17. How do or will you make money? How much could you make?
18. Which category best applies to your company?
19. If you had any other ideas you considered applying with, please list them.

---

## Equity Section

20. Have you formed ANY legal entity yet?
21. Have you taken any investment yet?
22. Are you currently fundraising?

---

## Curious Section

23. What convinced you to apply to Y Combinator?
24. How did you hear about Y Combinator?

## Notes on the "Other Ideas" question (#19)

YC asks this to understand how you think and whether you have conviction in the idea you're submitting. A confident answer shows you considered alternatives and chose this one deliberately. A weak answer is either empty ("this is our only idea") or lists alternatives that are more compelling than the primary application — which raises doubts about commitment.

## Notes on category (#18)

Pick the most specific category that applies. "B2B SaaS" is almost always technically accurate but often too broad. If you are a vertical SaaS, pick the vertical. If you are infrastructure, say so. The category affects which partners review your application.
