# Location — "Where do you live now, and where would the company be based after YC?"

Also covers: "Explain your decision regarding location."

---

## What YC is actually evaluating

YC's batch is in person in San Francisco. The location question is primarily checking: will you actually be in SF? If you're outside the US, it's also checking for visa awareness. The "explain your decision" follow-up is looking for strategic thinking about why your current location makes sense for the business — not a defense of remote work as a philosophy.

This is not a trick question. Answer it directly.

---

## What strong answers contain

- A direct answer to where founders live now and where the company will be based post-YC
- "We'll be in San Francisco for the full batch" — if true, say it clearly
- If currently outside SF: a specific, realistic relocation plan ("We're in Berlin, will move to SF for the batch, planning to stay 6–12 months to build customer relationships")
- If outside the US: acknowledgment of visa requirements and awareness of the timeline
- If staying in a different city: a compelling strategic reason that benefits the business (major customer base, specific talent market, regulatory advantage)

---

## What weak answers look like

- "We'll figure out the logistics" — no plan
- A long essay about why remote work is the future — doesn't answer the question YC actually asked
- Ignoring the relocation question entirely
- Visa uncertainty with no evidence of having looked into it
- "We plan to be in SF" with no acknowledgment of what that requires if they're currently elsewhere

---

## Probing questions to ask the founder

1. "Will you be in San Francisco in person for the full YC batch — yes or no?"
2. "If you're not already in SF, what's your specific relocation plan? Have you looked at housing?"
3. "If you're outside the US, have you researched the visa requirements for the batch?"
4. "Is there a strategic reason you're currently in [location] that YC should know?"
5. "What's your plan for the company's base after the batch — stay in SF or return?"

---

## Evidence to request from the founder's local files

- Generally none needed — this is a logistics and planning question
- If the founder claims their current location is strategic (customer proximity, talent market), ask them to name specific customers or hires that validate that claim
