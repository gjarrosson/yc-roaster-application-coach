# Curious — "What convinced you to apply to Y Combinator?"

Also covers: "How did you hear about Y Combinator?"

---

## What YC is actually evaluating

This is the lowest-stakes section of the application. YC is not making major decisions based on this answer — they're checking for basic authenticity and self-awareness. They want to see that you understand what YC actually provides beyond the check, and that you have a genuine reason for wanting to be in the program. Generic answers ("YC is the best accelerator") don't hurt you, but a specific, honest answer can add a small positive signal.

---

## What strong answers contain

- A specific reason for YC beyond the money: the network, the alumni community, the batch experience, the specific partners, the "do things that don't scale" ethos
- Optional: a specific YC company or alumni story that influenced the decision
- "How did you hear" answered honestly — it's genuinely just a logistics question for YC's own tracking

---

## What weak answers look like

- "YC is the best accelerator in the world" — generic, adds nothing
- "We need the funding" — honest but signals you're optimizing for the check rather than the program
- "A friend told me to apply" with no elaboration on why YC specifically

---

## Probing questions to ask the founder

1. "What specifically about how YC operates do you think would help you most at this stage?"
2. "Is there a specific YC company, founder, or essay that shaped how you think about building?"
3. "What would you do differently in the next 6 months if you get into YC vs. if you don't?"

---

## Evidence to request from the founder's local files

None needed. This is about motivation and genuine interest, not external evidence.

---

## Coaching note

Don't over-engineer this answer. A genuine two to three sentences outperforms a long essay every time. The answer should sound like something a real person would say, not a carefully optimized marketing message. If the founder has a genuine connection to a specific YC founder or company, that's worth mentioning — it shows they've thought about this beyond the prestige signal.
