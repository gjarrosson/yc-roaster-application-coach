# Product Description — "What is your company going to make?"

Full question: "Please describe your product and what it does or will do."

---

## What YC is actually evaluating

YC wants to know the specific thing you're building. Not the market. Not the problem. The product — what it is, what a user does with it on day one, and what exists right now vs. what's planned. The most common failure here is describing the problem in detail and then vaguely gesturing at a solution ("we're building a platform that addresses this"). Reviewers notice when founders write more about the pain than the product, and they flag it as a sign the product isn't real yet.

---

## What strong answers contain

- A description of what a user literally does in the product — the core workflow
- What exists today that someone could use right now (present tense, specific)
- What's being built next (roadmap, tied to the current progress, not a wishlist)
- The form factor: is it a web app, API, mobile app, physical device, script?
- Who the user is — specific enough that you could name them
- How the product delivers value: what changes for the user after they use it?

---

## What weak answers look like

- More paragraphs about the problem than about the product
- "We are building a platform that allows companies to X" — "platform" is a vague noun; "allows" is doing no work
- "Our AI-powered solution enables seamless X" — every adjective here is meaningless
- All roadmap, no present: "We will build X, then Y, then Z" with no mention of what exists now
- The answer contradicts the 50-char description — different products described in each
- The product described requires technology that doesn't exist yet without acknowledging that

---

## Probing questions to ask the founder

1. "What does a user actually do on day one of using this? Walk me through the exact steps."
2. "What exists today that I could open right now and use? Show me or describe it specifically."
3. "What is the single most important feature — the one thing that makes this useful at all?"
4. "If you ran a 60-second demo, what would you show? In what order?"
5. "Is the product described here consistent with what you described in your 50-char answer?"

---

## Evidence to request from the founder's local files

- README — product overview section; often has the clearest description of what was actually built
- Recent commit history — what features have been shipped in the last 60 days
- Any demo recording or link
- User-facing docs or onboarding flow — shows what users see and do
- For code-heavy products: architecture diagram or top-level module descriptions
