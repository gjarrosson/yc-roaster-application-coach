# Equity — Legal entity, investment, and fundraising

Covers three questions:
- "Have you formed ANY legal entity yet?"
- "Have you taken any investment yet?"
- "Are you currently fundraising?"

---

## What YC is actually evaluating

YC is checking for cap table issues that would complicate their investment, and for basic company hygiene. They invest via a standard SAFE at a fixed valuation cap — anything that makes that harder (messy cap table, conflicting investor terms, no incorporation) creates friction. This section is mostly a hygiene check, not a differentiation opportunity. The goal is a clean, direct answer with no surprises.

---

## What strong answers contain

- **Legal entity**: clear yes/no, with entity type and state of incorporation ("Delaware C-Corp, incorporated March 2024")
- **Equity split**: clear founder equity percentages, with vesting schedule noted ("70/30 split, 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff, started vesting January 2024")
- **Investment**: if taken, name the amount, type (SAFE, priced round, convertible note), and the valuation cap or discount
- **Fundraising**: direct yes/no; if yes, state the round size, target, and current status
- **No surprises**: if there are complicated terms or non-standard structures, disclose and explain them briefly

---

## What weak answers look like

- "We plan to incorporate" — not incorporated means YC would need to wait, which reduces their interest
- Complicated cap table with many small angel investors and no lead
- "We have a verbal agreement with an investor" — verbal is not real; only signed documents count
- Unclear equity split between founders, especially without vesting ("we split it 50/50")
- "We haven't discussed equity yet" — founders who haven't aligned on this create risk; fix this before applying
- Significant outside investment at a valuation that might make YC's standard terms difficult

---

## Probing questions to ask the founder

1. "Are you incorporated? What entity type, what state, what date?"
2. "What's the cap table right now? Who owns what percentage, with vesting?"
3. "Have you taken any money? From whom, what amount, what type of instrument, and what were the terms?"
4. "Is there anything in your current cap table or prior agreements that a YC investment might conflict with?"
5. "If you're fundraising: what's the round size, how much is committed, and what's your timeline?"

---

## Evidence to request from the founder's local files

- Incorporation documents (entity type, state, date — not the full docs, just the facts)
- Cap table summary: founder names, percentages, vesting schedules, any other shareholders
- Any SAFEs, convertible notes, or equity agreements with key terms (cap, discount, MFN)

---

## Coaching note on vesting

Founders with no vesting schedule on their equity are a red flag to sophisticated investors including YC. It means if one founder leaves early, they walk away with a large chunk of the company. If founders haven't set up vesting yet, they should do it before submitting — and then answer this question accurately.
