# Traction — "Are people using your product?" + "Do you have revenue?"

---

## What YC is actually evaluating

Traction is the single highest-signal section for most applications. It is the closest thing to proof that the idea works, that the market wants it, and that this team can execute. YC calibrates very carefully here: they've seen thousands of applications with inflated traction claims and they know exactly what real traction looks like vs. vanity metrics.

The goal of this section is to show **pull from the world** — that customers are coming to you, using the product repeatedly, and paying or willing to pay.

---

## What strong answers contain

**For post-launch companies:**
- Specific numbers with denominators: "47 paying customers, $8K MRR, 15% MoM growth over 4 months"
- Retention signal: "80% of users who activated in month 1 returned in month 3"
- Revenue quality: recurring subscriptions, low churn, expanding accounts
- Specific paying customer descriptions: company size, use case, how they found you
- Growth curve, not just current state: show the direction and rate of change
- Organic pull: how users are finding you (word of mouth, inbound, referrals)

**For pre-launch companies:**
- Named user discovery: "talked to 40 potential customers at [specific types of companies], here's what we learned"
- Credible demand commitments: signed LOIs, deposits, design partner agreements (named if possible)
- Waitlist with conversion signal — not just how many people signed up but what percentage activated
- Prototype testing with behavioral data: "8 out of 10 target users completed the core task; avg session was 12 minutes"
- Speed and velocity: how fast you're moving, how quickly you're learning

---

## What weak answers look like

- "10,000 users" with no retention, activation, or engagement context
- Waitlist numbers only: "5,000 people signed up" — what percentage converted to active users?
- "Our beta users love it" — sentiment without data
- Paid acquisition with no retention: "we spent $500 on ads and got 200 signups" — what happened after?
- A Fortune 500 company "expressed interest" — one anecdote is not a pattern
- Revenue projections: "we expect $1M ARR by year end" — this is a model, not traction
- Testimonials without usage data

---

## Probing questions to ask the founder

1. "What's your retention rate at 30 days? At 90 days?"
2. "How many of your users are paying? What do they pay? How long have they been paying?"
3. "Where did your users come from — organic, paid, referral? What's the conversion rate from each?"
4. "Name one customer who would do a reference call with a YC partner right now. What would they say?"
5. "If pre-launch: who specifically have you talked to? What did they say? Did any of them ask you to build this before you built it?"
6. "What's your MoM growth rate over the last 3 months? Not projected — actual."

---

## Evidence to request from the founder's local files

- **Analytics export** (CSV or JSON): DAU, WAU, MAU, retention cohorts — even a screenshot of a dashboard with real data
- **Revenue records**: MRR, ARR, churn data, payment history from Stripe or equivalent
- **Customer list** (can be anonymized): number of customers, when they signed, what they pay
- **LOIs or signed contracts**: even draft versions show credibility
- **Customer interview notes**: what users said in their own words — the specific quotes, not the paraphrase
- Any **NPS or CSAT data** if available
